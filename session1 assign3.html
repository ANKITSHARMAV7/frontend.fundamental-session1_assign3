Mass–energy equivalence

In physics, mass–energy equivalence is a concept formulated by Albert Einstein that explains the relationship between mass and energy. It states every mass has an energy equivalent and vice versa—expressed using the formula E = mc2 where E is the energy of a physical system, m is the mass of the system, and c is the speed of light in a vacuum


Dalton's gaseous law
